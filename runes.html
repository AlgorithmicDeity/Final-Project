Runes & Divination

Discover the mysteries of the Elder Futhark and the art of divination in the Norse tradition.

Traditional Norse Mythological Context

The Elder Futhark is the oldest known runic alphabet, used by Germanic tribes from approximately the 2nd to 8th centuries CE. The name "Futhark" derives from the first six runes: Fehu, Uruz, Thurisaz, Ansuz, Raidho, and Kenaz. This ancient system of symbols was not just a method of communication but also a sacred system for divination, magic, and spiritual connection.

According to Norse mythology, the runes were discovered by the Allfather Odin after he sacrificed himself to gain their wisdom. Odin hung for nine days and nights on the great World Tree, Yggdrasil, pierced by his own spear, without food or water. In his agony, the runes revealed themselves to him as symbols of knowledge and power.

Each rune carries a unique sound, symbolic meaning, and spiritual energy. The Elder Futhark consists of 24 runes, divided into three groups of eight called Ættir, each associated with specific deities and themes.

Symbolism and Structure of the Elder Futhark

The Elder Futhark reflects core themes of Norse cosmology, such as fate, balance, and the interconnectedness of life. Each rune serves as a symbol of:

  • Cosmic Forces: The runes represent natural and divine forces, tied to the elements of earth, water, fire, and air.
  • Magic and Divination: Runes were inscribed for blessings, protection, and predicting the future.
  • Wisdom and Knowledge: As Odin’s gift to humanity, runes symbolize the pursuit of higher understanding and guidance.
  • Power and Intention: Each rune carries energy that can be harnessed for rituals and spellwork.

The Three Ættir of the Elder Futhark

The 24 runes are divided into three Ættir (families), each overseen by a specific set of deities and representing different aspects of existence:

  1. Freyr’s Ætt (First Eight Runes): Associated with fertility, prosperity, and the natural world.
    • Fehu (ᚠ) - Wealth and abundance.
    • Uruz (ᚢ) - Strength and vitality.
    • Thurisaz (ᚦ) - Protection and conflict.
    • Ansuz (ᚨ) - Communication and divine wisdom.
    • Raidho (ᚱ) - Journey and movement.
    • Kenaz (ᚲ) - Knowledge and creativity.
    • Gebo (ᚷ) - Gifts and partnership.
    • Wunjo (ᚹ) - Joy and harmony.
  2. Heimdall’s Ætt (Second Eight Runes): Represents challenges, transformation, and the forces of chaos and order.
    • Hagalaz (ᚺ) - Disruption and natural forces.
    • Nauthiz (ᚾ) - Need and resistance.
    • Isa (ᛁ) - Stillness and patience.
    • Jera (ᛃ) - Harvest and cycles.
    • Eihwaz (ᛇ) - Endurance and transformation.
    • Perthro (ᛈ) - Mystery and fate.
    • Algiz (ᛉ) - Protection and higher self.
    • Sowilo (ᛋ) - Success and victory.
  3. Tyr’s Ætt (Final Eight Runes): Focuses on justice, legacy, and the spiritual journey.
    • Tiwaz (ᛏ) - Justice and sacrifice.
    • Berkano (ᛒ) - Growth and renewal.
    • Ehwaz (ᛖ) - Partnership and progress.
    • Mannaz (ᛗ) - Humanity and self-awareness.
    • Laguz (ᛚ) - Water and flow.
    • Ingwaz (ᛜ) - Fertility and potential.
    • Dagaz (ᛞ) - Awakening and clarity.
    • Othala (ᛟ) - Heritage and home.

Divination Practices for Norse Paganism and Grey Witchcraft

Divination serves as a bridge between the material world and the unseen forces that influence it. Whether consulting ancestors, spirits, deities, or simply tapping into your own intuition, divination can provide guidance, clarity, and insight. In Grey Witchcraft, these tools are viewed as neutral—what matters is how you wield them and the intention you set. Below are methods that align divination with Norse cosmology and the Grey Path.

1. Runes (Runic Divination)

Best Time to Use: When seeking wisdom, clarity about decisions, or deeper insight into a challenge. Especially powerful during significant Norse holidays like Yule, Imbolc, or solstices.

How to Use Runes:

  • Casting Runes:
    • Choose a set of Elder Futhark runes. Sit quietly, clear your mind, and focus on your question.
    • Hold the runes in a cloth bag, shake gently, and scatter them on a cloth or natural surface.
    • Interpret the runes that land face-up, noting their positions and relationships to each other.
  • Single Rune Draw: Draw one rune while meditating on your question. Reflect on its meaning in the context of Norse mythology and your current challenge.
  • Specific Norse Focus: Frame questions around Norse cosmology, such as:
    • "What wisdom do the Norns have for me today?"
    • "How can I embrace balance between chaos and creation like Loki and Freyja?"

2. Tarot Cards

Best Time to Use: For introspection, shadow work, or major life transitions when you need symbolic guidance.

How to Align Tarot with Norse Paganism and Grey Witchcraft:

  • Shadow Work Spreads: Create spreads inspired by Norse teachings. For example:
    • Loki’s Spread: “What truth am I avoiding?” “What needs transformation?” “How can I embrace this challenge?”
    • Freyja’s Spread: “Where do I find beauty and strength?” “What must I release to find balance?”
  • Runes and Tarot Together: Use a rune alongside tarot spreads to deepen interpretations.
  • Seasonal Guidance: Align readings with the Norse calendar, such as Yule or Midsummer, for focused insights.

3. Crystal Pendulums

Best Time to Use: For “yes/no” questions, locating lost items, or seeking confirmation on intuitive nudges.

How to Use Crystal Pendulums:

  • Guided by Intuition: Hold your pendulum, meditate, and connect with ancestors, deities, or nature for guidance.
  • Yes/No Questions:
    • Program your pendulum to show “yes” and “no” responses, then ask questions for clarity.
  • Locating Objects or Energies: Use pendulums to scan for missing objects, energy hotspots, or hidden items.

4. Dowsing Rods (Witching Rods)

Best Time to Use: For locating objects, energy fields, or directional guidance.

How to Use Dowsing Rods:

  • Yes/No Guidance: Hold the rods steady, ask a question, and observe their movement.
  • Finding Energy Lines: Use rods outdoors to find energy hotspots, sacred spaces, or ley lines.
  • Locating Objects: Ask for guidance to a lost item or location and follow the rods as they cross or diverge.

5. Body Divination (Intuition and Signs)

Best Time to Use: Daily life, quick decisions, or when tools are unavailable.

How to Practice Body Divination:

  • Swaying Method: Ask a question, stand still, and observe your body’s natural sway (forward for yes, backward for no).
  • Listening to Nature: Pay attention to signs like animal behavior, wind patterns, or sudden intuitive nudges. Example: Ravens appearing may symbolize guidance or a message.
  • Muscle Testing: Use muscle tension to test intuitive decisions. If you feel strong, it aligns; weakness suggests resistance.

When to Use Each Form of Divination

  • Runes: Deep wisdom, fate, and seasonal insights.
  • Tarot: Introspection, shadow work, and emotional clarity.
  • Crystal Pendulums: Quick answers, energy work, and scanning.
  • Dowsing Rods: Locating objects, sacred spaces, or energy imbalances.
  • Body Divination: Real-time decisions and deepening intuitive awareness.

In Grey Witchcraft, divination is a tool for empowerment, not dependency. Whether you feel drawn to one method or explore them all, the key is to trust your intuition. Norse Paganism teaches that the Norns weave our fates, but we hold the threads—divination helps illuminate the path forward.

"Honor your instincts, respect the tools, and let your shadow and light guide you."
